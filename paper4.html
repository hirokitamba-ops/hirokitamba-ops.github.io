The Tri-Layer Integrated Model (TLIM): A Structural Framework for Predicting Institutional Mobility

Author: Hiroki Tamba

Published: February 14, 2026

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18637708

ORCID: 0009-0004-7635-0741

Abstract

Institutional behavior is frequently analyzed through single-layer analytical frameworks focusing on legal design, policy processes, or historical path dependence. While each approach provides partial explanatory power, they often fail to account for the recurring empirical divergence between formal institutional design and observed institutional behavior. This paper introduces the Tri-Layer Integrated Model (TLIM), a structural analytical framework that conceptualizes institutional outcomes as emergent properties of interactions among three analytically distinct yet interdependent layers: the Textual layer (formal rules and codified constraints), the Ritual layer (operational practices and governance procedures), and the Genealogical layer (historically accumulated institutional structures and path-dependent constraints). By modeling institutional mobility as a function of multi-layer alignment patterns, TLIM provides a predictive framework capable of diagnosing institutional rigidity, semi-rigidity, and adaptive capacity across governance domains. Cross-domain structural validation demonstrates that institutional behavior cannot be reliably inferred from formal rule design alone, and that layer misalignment frequently creates hidden reform leverage points. The framework establishes a unified analytical architecture for comparative institutional analysis and provides a foundation for cumulative empirical research on institutional change and governance system adaptability.
