Abstract

This preprint introduces the Tri-Layer Integrated Model (TLIM) as a framework for analysing institutional systems across three interacting layers: textual representations, operational practices, and genealogical structures. The model is designed to capture how institutional continuity and transformation emerge from the interaction of these layers. Rather than focusing on isolated elements such as rules or behaviour, TLIM provides a systematic approach to understanding how institutional mobility is produced through layered interactions. The framework enables comparative analysis across domains by identifying patterns of stability, drift, and transformation. This paper presents the foundational definition of TLIM and establishes its analytical scope as a general model for studying knowledge systems and institutional dynamics.
