Abstract

This paper provides the foundational definition of the TLIM (Tri-Layer Integrated Model) research program. The TLIM framework integrates three analytical dimensions — predictive classification, institutional causation, and semantic governance — into a unified research architecture for investigating how contemporary governance systems operate through distributed technical, organizational, and cognitive infrastructures. The program establishes a coordinated theoretical foundation linking the production of causation through classification systems, the infrastructuralization of accountability across automated decision architectures, and the structural organization of cognition as a domain of governance. This document defines the scope, structure, and intellectual commitments of the TLIM Research Program and serves as the canonical reference for all associated publications.
